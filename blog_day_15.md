When you are buying a mattress in India, you are inevitably faced with a choice: Do you go with the modern, high-tech D2C efficiency of **Wakefit**, or the decades-old trusted comfort of **Sleepwell**? As an independent consultant with 22 years in the industry, I have performed technical teardowns on both. Here is what you need to know before you swipe your card.

### 1. Wakefit: The Data-Driven Budget King
Wakefit disrupted the market by using high-density PU foam and efficient logistics.
*   **The Build:** Their "Ortho Plus" series relies heavily on multi-layered foam. It’s excellent for younger sleepers (under 30) who have no chronic spinal issues.
*   **The Pros:** Incredible value for money, reliable shipping, and a modern "Mattress-in-a-Box" experience.
*   **The Catch:** Memory foam can trap heat in South Indian summers. If you are a "hot sleeper," Wakefit’s standard foam may cause night sweats.

### 2. Sleepwell: The Legacy Comfort Authority
Sleepwell is a household name for a reason. Their manufacturing scale is unmatched.
*   **The Build:** They use proprietary "Nexagel" and "Resitec" foams. Their Nexa series is designed to behave like memory foam but with better responsiveness—meaning you don't feel "stuck" in the bed.
*   **The Pros:** Massive physical dealer network (you can touch it before you buy), certified "Ortho" ranges, and superior edge support in their premium models.
*   **The Catch:** You pay a premium for the brand name and the physical showroom overheads.

### Sandeep’s Expert Verdict
*   **Choose Wakefit if:** You are on a budget, renting an apartment, or buying for a guest room. It is the best "Utility" mattress in India.
*   **Choose Sleepwell if:** You are buying your "Forever Bed," value legacy durability, or are a senior citizen who needs high-responsiveness foam that makes it easy to get out of bed.
