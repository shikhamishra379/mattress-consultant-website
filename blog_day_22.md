Sciatica is not just a back problem; it’s a nerve problem. When your mattress sags even by half an inch, it puts direct pressure on the sciatic nerve, causing radiating pain from your lower back down to your legs. 

As a consultant with 22 years of experience, I’ve found that while the right mattress is the long-term cure, your **Sleep Position** is the immediate fix.

### 1. The "Side-Lying" Recovery Position
If you sleep on your side, your top leg can often slide forward, rotating your pelvis and "pinching" the sciatic nerve.
*   **The Fix:** Place a firm, high-density pillow between your knees. This keeps your hips, pelvis, and spine in perfect alignment, taking the tension off the nerve.

### 2. The "Back-Sleeping" Elevation
Sleeping flat on your back can sometimes increase the arch in your lower back, leading to compression.
*   **The Fix:** Place a small pillow or a rolled-up towel under your knees. This flattens the lumbar curve and opens up the spinal canal, providing instant relief for L4-L5 issues.

### 3. The "Cradle" (For Severe Flare-ups)
If the pain is acute, sleeping in a slightly reclined position can help. 
*   **The Fix:** Use a wedge pillow to elevate your upper body and another under your knees. This mimics the zero-gravity position, which is the gold standard for spinal decompression.

### Sandeep’s Expert Audit for Sciatica
If you have Sciatica, you **must** avoid soft memory foam. You need a "Surface of Resilience" (Natural Latex or SmartGrid) that pushes back against your weight to keep the nerve path clear.
