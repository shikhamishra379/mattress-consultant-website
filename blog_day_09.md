Most people walk into a showroom and choose a mattress because it feels "nice" for 30 seconds. But as an independent consultant with 22 years of experience, I can tell you that the most critical factor in your long-term comfort isn't the brand—it's your **Body Mass Index (BMI)**.

### Why BMI Matters: The "Support Factor"
Your weight determines how deep you sink into the mattress layers. If you are a heavy sleeper (90kg+) and you buy a standard "soft" memory foam mattress designed for a 60kg person, you will "bottom out" within 6 months.

1. **Low BMI (Under 18.5):** You need more contouring. A soft memory foam or a plush hybrid will provide the pressure relief your lighter frame requires.
2. **Standard BMI (18.5 - 25):** You are the industry standard. Most "Medium-Firm" mattresses will work for you, but material choice still depends on your sleeping position.
3. **High BMI (Over 25 / 90kg+):** You need **High-Density Support**. Look for 40D+ foam density or thick pocket springs with a lower gauge (thicker wire). If you choose a low-density mattress, it will develop a permanent sag within 2 years.

### Sandeep’s Expert Tip for Heavy Sleepers
Don't be fooled by "10-inch thickness." A 6-inch high-density mattress often provides better support than a 10-inch low-density "fluffy" bed. Always ask for the **GSM** of the fabric and the **Density (kg/m3)** of the core foam.
