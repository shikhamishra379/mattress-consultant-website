### 1. Bonnell Springs: The Old Guard
Bonnell coils are hour-glass shaped and interconnected with a metal wire. 
*   **The Problem:** Because the springs are tied together, when one moves, they all move. This is called "Motion Transfer."
*   **Durability:** They are very durable and cheap, but they tend to get noisy (squeaky) over time as the metal-to-metal connections wear down.
*   **Best For:** Guest rooms or budget setups where motion isolation isn't a priority.

### 2. Pocket Springs: The Modern Standard
Pocket springs (also called Encased Coils) are individual springs wrapped in their own fabric pocket.
*   **The Solution:** Because each spring moves independently, you can have "Zero Motion Transfer." If your partner moves on the other side, you feel nothing.
*   **Contouring:** Pocket springs adapt to the specific pressure of your shoulders and hips much better than Bonnell coils, providing superior spinal alignment.
*   **Best For:** Couples, side sleepers, and anyone with chronic back pain.

### Sandeep’s Expert Verdict
For a primary bedroom, **Pocket Springs are non-negotiable**. However, not all pocket springs are equal. Always ask about the "Wire Gauge"—thicker wires provide firmer support for heavy sleepers (90kg+).
