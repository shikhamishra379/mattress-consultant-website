Cervical Spondylosis is more than just "neck pain." It is a degenerative condition of the cervical spine that can lead to dizziness, headaches, and radiating arm pain if your sleep environment is working against you. ### The Science of "Spinal Neutral Alignment"
If you have Cervical Spondylosis, the goal is to keep your neck in a straight line with the rest of your spine. 
1. **The Mattress Role:** If your mattress is too soft, your hips sink, causing your upper back to curve and your neck to tilt upwards. If the mattress is too hard, your shoulders don't sink, forcing your neck into an unnatural angle.
2. **The Pillow Role:** The pillow's only job is to fill the gap between your ear and the mattress (if you sleep on your side) or your neck and the mattress (if you sleep on your back).

### Sandeep’s Expert Recommendation
*   **The Best Mattress:** A Medium-Firm Zoned Hybrid or Natural Latex mattress. These materials provide the necessary "push-back" to keep your hips level, ensuring your neck starts from a flat foundation.
*   **The Best Pillow:** Memory foam "Contour" pillows are excellent, but only if the height (loft) matches your shoulder width.
